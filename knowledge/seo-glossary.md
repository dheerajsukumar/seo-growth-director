# Core Terms

## SEO

**Definition**

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is the practice of improving a website's visibility, relevance, user experience, and authority to increase qualified organic traffic from search engines and AI-powered search systems.

---

## Organic Traffic

**Definition**

Visitors who arrive from unpaid search engine results.

---

## Search Intent

**Definition**

The underlying objective a user wants to accomplish when performing a search query.

---

## Entity

**Definition**

A uniquely identifiable person, organization, place, product, service, concept, or thing that search engines and AI systems can understand independently of keywords.

---

## Semantic SEO

**Definition**

The practice of optimizing content around topics, entities, relationships, and contextual meaning rather than relying solely on keyword optimization.

---

## Topical Authority

**Definition**

The level of expertise and completeness a website demonstrates across an entire subject area through comprehensive, interconnected content.

---

## Knowledge Graph

**Definition**

A structured network of entities and relationships used by search engines and AI systems to understand and organize information.

---

## AI Search

**Definition**

Search experiences powered by artificial intelligence that retrieve, synthesize, and generate answers rather than simply ranking web pages.
---

## Crawlability

**Definition**

The ability of search engine crawlers to discover and access pages on a website through links, sitemaps, and internal navigation.

---

## Indexability

**Definition**

The ability of a page to be stored in a search engine's index and become eligible to appear in search results.

---

## Crawl Budget

**Definition**

The number of pages a search engine crawler is willing and able to crawl on a website within a given period.

---

## Canonical Tag

**Definition**

An HTML element that identifies the preferred version of a page when multiple URLs contain similar or duplicate content.

---

## Robots.txt

**Definition**

A text file that provides crawling instructions to search engine bots by specifying which sections of a website may or may not be crawled.

---

## XML Sitemap

**Definition**

A structured file that lists important URLs on a website to help search engines discover and crawl content more efficiently.

---

## Structured Data

**Definition**

Standardized machine-readable markup, typically using Schema.org vocabulary, that helps search engines and AI systems understand page content and entities.

---

## Rich Results

**Definition**

Enhanced search results that display additional information such as ratings, FAQs, products, recipes, or business details through structured data.

---

## Core Web Vitals

**Definition**

A set of user experience metrics that measure loading performance, interaction responsiveness, and visual stability using LCP, INP, and CLS.

---

## E-E-A-T

**Definition**

Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness—Google's quality framework for evaluating the credibility of content, authors, and organizations.
---

## Hreflang

**Definition**

An HTML attribute that tells search engines which language and regional version of a page should be served to users in different countries or languages.

---

## Internal Linking

**Definition**

The practice of linking pages within the same website to improve navigation, distribute authority, strengthen topical relationships, and assist search engine crawling.

---

## Topic Cluster

**Definition**

A collection of interconnected content centered around a primary topic, typically consisting of a pillar page supported by related articles and resources.

---

## Entity Relationship

**Definition**

The semantic connection between two or more entities that helps search engines and AI systems understand context and meaning.

---

## Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)

**Definition**

A Core Web Vitals metric that measures the time required for the largest visible content element to load within the user's viewport.

---

## Interaction to Next Paint (INP)

**Definition**

A Core Web Vitals metric that measures how quickly a webpage responds to user interactions throughout the page lifecycle.

---

## Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)

**Definition**

A Core Web Vitals metric that measures unexpected visual movement of page elements during loading.

---

## Google Business Profile

**Definition**

Google's business listing platform that enables organizations to manage their presence across Google Search and Google Maps.

---

## AI Overview

**Definition**

An AI-generated summary presented within Google Search that synthesizes information from multiple sources to answer a user's query.

---

## Knowledge Panel

**Definition**

An information panel displayed by search engines that summarizes key facts about a recognized entity using Knowledge Graph data.
---

# Glossary Usage Guidelines

The SEO Glossary serves as the single source of truth for terminology used throughout the SEO Growth Director.

Every workflow, template, report, audit, and recommendation should reference these standardized definitions to ensure consistency across all outputs.

When new SEO concepts emerge, they should be added to this glossary before being incorporated into workflows or templates.

---

# Glossary Maintenance Principles

The SEO Growth Director should maintain glossary entries that are:

- Accurate
- Concise
- Vendor-neutral
- Technology-agnostic where possible
- Consistent with current SEO best practices
- Relevant to AI-powered search
- Easy to understand
- Easy to reference

Definitions should evolve only when industry standards or search technologies change significantly.

---

# Cross-Referencing

Whenever possible, workflows should reference glossary terms instead of redefining them.

Examples include:

- Technical SEO → Crawlability, Indexability, Canonical Tag
- Content Strategy → Search Intent, Topical Authority
- Semantic SEO → Entity, Entity Relationship, Knowledge Graph
- AI Search → AI Overview, Structured Data, Knowledge Graph
- Local SEO → Google Business Profile
- International SEO → Hreflang
- Performance → LCP, INP, CLS

This improves consistency and simplifies future maintenance.

---

# Guiding Principle

A shared vocabulary creates a shared understanding.

The SEO Growth Director should use consistent terminology across every recommendation, workflow, audit, template, and report to ensure clarity, professionalism, and high-quality decision making.

The glossary is a living knowledge asset that supports the long-term evolution of the entire SEO Growth Director.
