# Keyword Research

## Purpose

The Keyword Research knowledge base enables the SEO Growth Director to discover, evaluate, prioritize, and organize search opportunities based on business objectives, user intent, competition, and commercial value.

Rather than collecting keywords with high search volume, the objective is to identify search opportunities that generate sustainable organic growth and measurable business impact.

---

# Core Philosophy

Keyword research is the process of understanding how people search, what problems they are trying to solve, and how those searches align with business goals.

Every keyword recommendation should balance:

- Business objectives
- User intent
- Search demand
- Competition
- Commercial value
- Topical authority
- Long-term sustainability

Keyword research should always begin with business goals—not keyword tools.

---

# Keyword Research Objectives

The SEO Growth Director performs keyword research to:

1. Identify business opportunities.
2. Discover customer language.
3. Understand search demand.
4. Build topical authority.
5. Improve content planning.
6. Prioritize SEO efforts.
7. Support AI Search visibility.
8. Improve conversion potential.
9. Discover content gaps.
10. Create long-term SEO growth.
---

# Keyword Research Workflow

Every keyword research project follows this workflow.

```
Business Goals
      ↓
Audience Research
      ↓
Seed Keywords
      ↓
Keyword Expansion
      ↓
Search Intent
      ↓
Competition Analysis
      ↓
Commercial Evaluation
      ↓
Keyword Clustering
      ↓
Prioritization
      ↓
Content Planning
```

Keyword research should always begin with understanding the business—not the keyword tool.

---

# Seed Keywords

Seed keywords are the starting point for all keyword research.

Sources include:

- Products
- Services
- Customer interviews
- Sales calls
- Support tickets
- Competitor websites
- Internal search
- Google Search Console
- Industry terminology

Good seed keywords describe the business rather than isolated search phrases.

---

# Keyword Expansion

The SEO Growth Director expands keywords using:

- Google Autocomplete
- Related Searches
- People Also Ask
- Competitor analysis
- Semantic relationships
- Entity research
- Customer language
- AI-generated variations

The objective is comprehensive topic coverage rather than collecting unrelated keywords.

---

# Keyword Evaluation

Every keyword should be evaluated using:

- Search demand
- Business relevance
- Competition
- Commercial intent
- Conversion potential
- Topical fit
- AI Search value
- Content difficulty

High search volume alone should never determine keyword priority.
---

# Keyword Categories

The SEO Growth Director classifies keywords into the following categories.

## By Intent

- Informational
- Navigational
- Commercial
- Transactional

## By Length

- Head Keywords
- Body Keywords
- Long-tail Keywords

## By Business Value

- High-value keywords
- Supporting keywords
- Brand keywords
- Competitor keywords

## By Topic

- Primary topics
- Supporting topics
- Entity-based topics
- Question-based topics

Each keyword should belong to multiple categories to improve prioritization.

---

# Keyword Clustering

Keywords should be grouped into logical clusters rather than treated individually.

A cluster should include:

- Primary keyword
- Secondary keywords
- Semantic variations
- Related entities
- Questions
- Supporting topics

Clusters become the foundation for content planning and topical authority.

---

# Keyword Prioritization

Priority should be determined using:

- Business impact
- Search intent alignment
- Commercial value
- Competition level
- Current authority
- Resource requirements
- Expected ROI
- Long-term strategic value

The highest-priority keyword is not always the one with the highest search volume.

---

# Guiding Principle

Keyword research is the foundation of every successful SEO strategy.

The objective is not to rank for more keywords.

The objective is to rank for the right keywords that generate sustainable business growth.
