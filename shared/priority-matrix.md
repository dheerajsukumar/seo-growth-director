# Priority Matrix

## Purpose

The Priority Matrix provides a standardized framework for determining the implementation priority of SEO recommendations.

The objective is to ensure that recommendations are prioritized consistently based on business value, SEO impact, implementation effort, and implementation risk.

---

# Core Philosophy

Not every recommendation should be implemented immediately.

The SEO Growth Director should prioritize actions that deliver the greatest business value with the most efficient use of available resources.

Priority should always reflect business context rather than technical preference.

---

# Priority Objectives

The Priority Matrix enables the SEO Growth Director to:

1. Prioritize SEO initiatives consistently.
2. Balance impact and effort.
3. Improve implementation planning.
4. Support executive decision-making.
5. Maximize SEO ROI.
6. Improve resource allocation.
7. Reduce implementation risk.
8. Support long-term SEO growth.

---

# Priority Levels

Recommendations should be assigned one of the following priority levels:

## Critical

Characteristics:

- Severe business impact
- Severe SEO impact
- Immediate action required

Typical examples:

- Robots.txt blocking important pages
- Noindex applied to key landing pages
- Manual actions
- Major indexing failures

Recommended timeline:

Immediately
---

## High

Characteristics:

- High business impact
- High SEO impact
- Significant improvement opportunity
- Should be planned quickly

Typical examples:

- Missing canonical tags
- Large-scale duplicate content
- Poor Core Web Vitals
- Broken internal linking
- Missing structured data on key pages

Recommended timeline:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium

Characteristics:

- Moderate business impact
- Moderate SEO impact
- Improves overall website quality

Typical examples:

- Thin content
- Metadata improvements
- Navigation enhancements
- Image optimization
- Internal linking improvements

Recommended timeline:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low

Characteristics:

- Limited business impact
- Limited SEO impact
- Nice-to-have improvements

Typical examples:

- Minor metadata refinements
- Small URL improvements
- Cosmetic enhancements
- Minor schema additions

Recommended timeline:

As resources allow

---

# Prioritization Principles

When assigning priority, always consider:

- Business Impact
- SEO Impact
- User Impact
- AI Search Impact
- Implementation Effort
- Implementation Risk
- Expected ROI

Priority should never be determined by technical severity alone.
---

# Prioritization Workflow

Every recommendation should follow this prioritization process.

```
Identify Issue
        ↓
Collect Evidence
        ↓
Determine Root Cause
        ↓
Assess Business Impact
        ↓
Assess SEO Impact
        ↓
Assess User Impact
        ↓
Assess AI Search Impact
        ↓
Estimate Implementation Effort
        ↓
Estimate Implementation Risk
        ↓
Estimate Expected ROI
        ↓
Assign Priority
        ↓
Recommend Implementation Timeline
```

Recommendations should always follow this sequence before assigning priority.

---

# Escalation Rules

The SEO Growth Director should immediately escalate recommendations when:

- Search visibility has dropped significantly.
- Important pages are no longer indexed.
- Manual actions are detected.
- Security issues affect SEO.
- Website migrations introduce critical risks.
- Business-critical pages become inaccessible.
- Data is insufficient to make a confident recommendation.

Escalation should clearly explain:

- Why the issue is critical.
- Which business objectives are affected.
- Which stakeholders should be involved.
- Recommended immediate next steps.

---

# Guiding Principle

Priority should always be determined by business value rather than technical complexity.

The SEO Growth Director should recommend the sequence of work that delivers the greatest measurable improvement in business performance, search visibility, user experience, and AI Search readiness while minimizing implementation effort and risk.
