# Scoring Framework

## Purpose

The Scoring Framework provides a standardized methodology for evaluating SEO issues, opportunities, and recommendations.

The objective is to ensure that every workflow prioritizes actions consistently using measurable criteria rather than subjective judgment.

---

# Core Philosophy

Not every SEO issue deserves the same priority.

The SEO Growth Director should score issues based on their potential impact on business performance, search visibility, user experience, and implementation effort.

Scoring should enable objective decision-making.

---

# Scoring Objectives

The Scoring Framework should help the SEO Growth Director:

1. Prioritize recommendations consistently.
2. Compare multiple SEO issues objectively.
3. Estimate business impact.
4. Estimate SEO impact.
5. Evaluate implementation complexity.
6. Support executive decision-making.
7. Improve roadmap planning.
8. Standardize workflow outputs.

---

# Scoring Dimensions

Every recommendation should be evaluated using:

- Business Impact
- SEO Impact
- User Impact
- AI Search Impact
- Implementation Effort
- Implementation Risk
- Expected ROI

Each dimension should be assessed independently before determining overall priority.
---

# Scoring Scale

Every scoring dimension should use the following scale.

| Score | Meaning |
|-------:|---------|
| 1 | Very Low |
| 2 | Low |
| 3 | Moderate |
| 4 | High |
| 5 | Very High |

Use the same scoring scale across all workflows to maintain consistency.

---

# Business Impact

Business Impact measures how strongly an issue affects business performance.

Consider:

- Revenue
- Lead generation
- Customer acquisition
- Brand visibility
- Customer experience

Higher scores indicate greater business impact.

---

# SEO Impact

SEO Impact measures how strongly an issue affects search performance.

Consider:

- Crawlability
- Indexability
- Rankings
- Organic traffic
- AI Search visibility
- Entity understanding

Higher scores indicate greater SEO impact.

---

# User Impact

User Impact evaluates how strongly an issue affects website visitors.

Consider:

- User experience
- Navigation
- Accessibility
- Performance
- Content usability
- Trust

Higher scores indicate greater impact on users.

---

# AI Search Impact

AI Search Impact evaluates how strongly an issue influences visibility across AI-powered search systems.

Consider:

- Structured Data
- Entity optimization
- Semantic completeness
- Knowledge Graph signals
- AI citation potential
- Content quality

Higher scores indicate greater importance for AI-powered search.
---

# Implementation Effort

Implementation Effort estimates the level of work required to resolve an issue.

Consider:

- Development effort
- Content effort
- Design effort
- Testing requirements
- Deployment complexity
- Cross-team coordination

Scoring:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------:|---------|
| 1 | Very Easy |
| 2 | Easy |
| 3 | Moderate |
| 4 | Difficult |
| 5 | Very Difficult |

Lower effort with higher impact should generally receive higher priority.

---

# Implementation Risk

Implementation Risk estimates the likelihood of introducing unintended consequences.

Consider:

- Website stability
- SEO migration risk
- Data integrity
- User experience impact
- Business disruption
- Rollback complexity

Scoring:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------:|---------|
| 1 | Very Low Risk |
| 2 | Low Risk |
| 3 | Moderate Risk |
| 4 | High Risk |
| 5 | Very High Risk |

Recommendations should balance expected benefits with implementation risk.

---

# Expected ROI

Expected ROI estimates the potential return from implementing a recommendation.

Consider:

- Organic traffic growth
- Lead generation
- Revenue growth
- Customer acquisition
- Operational efficiency
- Long-term SEO value

Scoring:

| Score | Meaning |
|-------:|---------|
| 1 | Very Low ROI |
| 2 | Low ROI |
| 3 | Moderate ROI |
| 4 | High ROI |
| 5 | Very High ROI |

Recommendations with high ROI and low implementation effort should generally be prioritized.

---

# Overall Priority Score

The SEO Growth Director should determine overall priority by balancing:

- Business Impact
- SEO Impact
- User Impact
- AI Search Impact
- Implementation Effort
- Implementation Risk
- Expected ROI

The scoring framework should support consistent, transparent, and evidence-based prioritization.

---

# Guiding Principle

Scoring is a decision-support tool, not a substitute for professional judgment.

The SEO Growth Director should combine objective scoring with business context to recommend actions that deliver the greatest long-term value while remaining practical to implement.
