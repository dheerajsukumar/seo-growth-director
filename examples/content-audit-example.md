# Content Audit Example

## Purpose

This document demonstrates what an excellent Content Audit should look like when generated by the SEO Growth Director.

It serves as the reference implementation for:

- Content evaluation
- Search intent analysis
- Content quality assessment
- Business reasoning
- Recommendation quality
- Executive presentation

Every future Content Audit should aim to meet or exceed the quality demonstrated in this example.

# Executive Summary

Example Company

B2B SaaS

## Executive Summary

The website contains a strong library of informational content but lacks topical depth in several commercially important areas. Multiple pages compete for similar keywords, several high-value articles are outdated, and content opportunities remain untapped across the buying journey.

Improving topical authority, consolidating overlapping pages, and refreshing outdated content is expected to increase organic visibility, improve conversion performance, and strengthen AI Search visibility.

Estimated implementation effort is 10–12 weeks.

# Content Audit Findings

## Finding 1

### Category

Content Freshness

### Priority

High

### Evidence

- 28% of blog articles have not been updated in over 24 months.
- Several statistics reference outdated industry reports.
- Multiple articles contain broken outbound links.

### Root Cause

A structured content maintenance process has not been established.

### Business Impact

- Reduced organic visibility
- Lower content trustworthiness
- Declining engagement
- Missed AI citation opportunities

### Recommendation

Implement a quarterly content refresh program focusing on high-performing pages first.

### Expected Outcome

- Improved rankings
- Increased engagement
- Higher AI Search visibility
- Better conversion performance

---

## Finding 2

### Category

Search Intent Alignment

### Priority

Critical

### Evidence

- Several commercial keywords target informational content.
- Product pages compete with blog articles for identical queries.
- High bounce rates on commercial landing pages.

### Root Cause

Content has been created without a structured keyword-to-intent mapping strategy.

### Business Impact

- Lower conversions
- Keyword cannibalization
- Reduced commercial visibility
- Inefficient customer journeys

### Recommendation

Map every primary keyword to the correct stage of the buyer journey and consolidate competing pages.

### Expected Outcome

- Improved keyword relevance
- Higher conversion rates
- Better internal linking
- Increased qualified traffic

# Content Improvement Roadmap

## Phase 1 – Immediate Improvements (Weeks 1–3)

Priority initiatives:

- Refresh outdated high-traffic articles
- Fix broken outbound links
- Update statistics and references
- Improve metadata for top-performing pages

Expected Outcome:

- Improved content freshness
- Better user engagement
- Increased search visibility

---

## Phase 2 – Strategic Content Optimization (Weeks 4–8)

Priority initiatives:

- Resolve keyword cannibalization
- Align content with search intent
- Strengthen internal linking
- Improve E-E-A-T signals
- Expand entity coverage

Expected Outcome:

- Higher rankings
- Improved conversion rates
- Stronger AI Search readiness

---

## Phase 3 – Topical Authority Growth (Weeks 9–12)

Priority initiatives:

- Build topic clusters
- Create supporting content
- Strengthen semantic relationships
- Develop content hubs
- Expand commercial content

Expected Outcome:

- Increased topical authority
- Better AI citation opportunities
- Sustainable long-term organic growth

# Key Takeaways

This example demonstrates the expected quality standard for every Content Audit produced by the SEO Growth Director.

Every content audit should:

- Present evidence before conclusions.
- Connect content quality to business outcomes.
- Prioritize high-impact recommendations.
- Include measurable success metrics.
- Provide a phased implementation roadmap.
- Support executive decision-making while remaining actionable for content and SEO teams.
