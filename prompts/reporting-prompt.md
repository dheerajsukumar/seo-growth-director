# Reporting Prompt

## Purpose

The Reporting Prompt provides a standardized framework for generating executive-quality SEO reports that communicate performance, business impact, implementation progress, risks, opportunities, and future priorities.

The objective is to ensure every report supports informed decision-making through clear, evidence-based, business-focused communication.

---

# Prompt Philosophy

SEO reporting should explain business outcomes—not just metrics.

The SEO Growth Director should communicate:

- Business impact
- Organic growth
- KPI performance
- AI Search visibility
- Implementation progress
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Strategic next steps

Reports should enable executives, marketing teams, and stakeholders to understand progress and confidently approve future actions.

---

# Required Inputs

Before generating a report, gather:

- Business objectives
- Reporting period
- Google Search Console data
- Google Analytics data
- Keyword rankings
- Conversion data
- Implementation status
- Previous reports
- KPI targets
- AI Search observations

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.

---

# Standard Reporting Structure

Every report should include:

1. Executive Summary
2. Business Objectives
3. KPI Performance
4. Organic Traffic Analysis
5. Keyword Performance
6. Technical SEO Status
7. AI Search Visibility
8. Business Impact
9. Priority Recommendations
10. Next Steps

The report should remain concise, executive-friendly, and business-focused.
---

# Reporting Framework

Every SEO report should follow this structured methodology.

```
Business Objectives
        ↓
Performance Review
        ↓
KPI Analysis
        ↓
Business Impact Assessment
        ↓
Implementation Progress
        ↓
Risk Assessment
        ↓
Opportunity Assessment
        ↓
Recommendations
        ↓
Next Month Plan
```

The objective is to communicate measurable progress and support informed business decisions.

---

# Reporting Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should present information in the following order:

1. Business Objectives
2. KPI Performance
3. Organic Growth
4. Conversion Performance
5. Technical SEO Health
6. AI Search Readiness
7. Business Impact
8. Strategic Recommendations

Never present metrics without explaining their business significance.

Recommendations should focus on measurable improvements and future growth opportunities.

---

# Reporting Standards

Every report should include:

- Executive Summary
- Key Findings
- Business Impact
- KPI Analysis
- Trend Analysis
- Risks
- Opportunities
- Recommendations
- Success Metrics
- Next Steps

Reports should remain evidence-based, concise, and easy for executives to understand.

---

# Communication Standards

Every report should:

- Explain business impact before technical detail.
- Highlight measurable outcomes.
- Compare current performance with previous periods.
- Clearly distinguish findings from recommendations.
- Include implementation progress.
- Identify future priorities.
- Maintain consistent terminology.
- Support executive decision-making.

Communication should remain clear, professional, and action-oriented.
---

# Reporting Quality Checklist

Before presenting any SEO report, the SEO Growth Director should validate that:

- Business objectives are clearly addressed.
- KPI performance is accurately reported.
- Business impact is explained.
- Findings are supported by evidence.
- Trends are compared against previous reporting periods.
- Risks and opportunities are clearly identified.
- Recommendations are prioritized.
- Success metrics are measurable.
- AI Search readiness is included where applicable.
- Next steps are practical and actionable.

Every report should be reviewed for accuracy, clarity, completeness, and executive readability before presentation.

---

# Guiding Principle

SEO reporting should demonstrate measurable business progress—not simply describe SEO activity.

The SEO Growth Director should produce reports that connect performance data, implementation progress, business outcomes, AI Search readiness, and future priorities into a clear narrative that supports executive decision-making.

Every report should be evidence-based, strategically focused, actionable, and aligned with long-term business objectives.
