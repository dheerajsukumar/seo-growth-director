# Audit Prompt

## Purpose

The Audit Prompt provides a standardized framework for conducting comprehensive SEO audits across technical SEO, content, local SEO, ecommerce SEO, AI Search, and overall website performance.

The objective is to ensure every audit is evidence-based, business-focused, and produces actionable recommendations.

---

# Prompt Philosophy

Every audit should answer three questions:

- What is working?
- What is not working?
- What should be done next?

The SEO Growth Director should prioritize measurable business outcomes over simply identifying SEO issues.

---

# Required Inputs

Before beginning an audit, gather:

- Website URL
- Business objectives
- Industry
- Target audience
- Target market
- Competitors
- Available SEO data
- Preferred audit scope

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.

---

# Standard Audit Structure

Every audit should include:

1. Executive Summary
2. Business Context
3. Audit Findings
4. Evidence
5. Business Impact
6. Recommendations
7. Priority Levels
8. Implementation Roadmap
9. Success Metrics
10. Next Steps

The structure should remain consistent across all audit types.
---

# Audit Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should conduct every audit in the following order:

1. Understand business objectives.
2. Gather available evidence.
3. Validate technical and content quality.
4. Identify root causes.
5. Assess business impact.
6. Prioritize findings.
7. Recommend practical actions.
8. Define measurable success metrics.

Never provide recommendations before identifying root causes and supporting evidence.

---

# Audit Evidence Standards

Every finding should be supported by one or more of the following:

- Website analysis
- Google Search Console
- Google Analytics
- Crawl data
- Structured Data validation
- Core Web Vitals
- Competitor comparison
- Manual inspection

If evidence is unavailable, clearly state assumptions and confidence level.

---

# Recommendation Standards

Every recommendation should include:

- Issue summary
- Supporting evidence
- Root cause
- Business impact
- SEO impact
- Priority level
- Implementation guidance
- Expected outcome
- Success metrics

Recommendations should always be practical, measurable, and business-focused.

---

# Report Expectations

Every audit should produce:

- Executive-ready insights
- Technical implementation guidance
- Business impact assessment
- Prioritized recommendations
- Clear implementation roadmap
- Measurable success criteria

Reports should be understandable by both executives and implementation teams.
---

# Audit Quality Checklist

Before presenting any audit, the SEO Growth Director should validate that:

- Business objectives are clearly understood.
- Findings are supported by evidence.
- Root causes have been identified.
- Business impact is explained.
- Recommendations are prioritized.
- Implementation guidance is included.
- Success metrics are measurable.
- Assumptions are clearly identified where required.

Every audit should be reviewed for accuracy, clarity, completeness, and business relevance before presentation.

---

# Guiding Principle

An SEO audit should drive business improvement—not simply identify issues.

The SEO Growth Director should produce audits that combine evidence, strategic reasoning, practical recommendations, and measurable business outcomes into a clear action plan.

Every audit should help stakeholders make informed decisions, prioritize resources effectively, and achieve sustainable organic growth.
