# Ecommerce SEO Audit

## Purpose

The Ecommerce SEO Audit workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to evaluate an ecommerce website's ability to attract qualified organic traffic, optimize product discoverability, improve category performance, strengthen AI Search readiness, and increase online revenue.

The objective is to identify opportunities that improve product visibility, user experience, and conversion performance across search engines and AI-powered shopping platforms.

---

# Audit Philosophy

Ecommerce SEO is about helping customers discover, evaluate, and purchase products with confidence.

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate:

- Product pages
- Category pages
- Site architecture
- Product entities
- Structured Data
- Internal linking
- User experience
- AI Shopping readiness

Recommendations should improve both search visibility and revenue generation.

---

# Audit Objectives

Every Ecommerce SEO Audit should:

1. Evaluate category page performance.
2. Review product page quality.
3. Assess product discoverability.
4. Validate product structured data.
5. Evaluate internal linking.
6. Review duplicate content.
7. Assess Core Web Vitals.
8. Evaluate AI Shopping readiness.
9. Identify revenue growth opportunities.
10. Produce a prioritized ecommerce SEO roadmap.

---

# Inputs Required

Before starting an Ecommerce SEO Audit, gather:

- Website URL
- Product catalog
- Category structure
- Business objectives
- Target audience
- Ecommerce platform
- Google Search Console data (if available)
- Google Analytics data (if available)
- Product feed (if available)
- Competitor ecommerce websites

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.
---

# Audit Framework

Every Ecommerce SEO Audit follows this structured methodology.

```
Business Objectives
        ↓
Category Analysis
        ↓
Product Page Analysis
        ↓
Technical Validation
        ↓
User Experience Assessment
        ↓
Revenue Opportunity Assessment
        ↓
AI Shopping Readiness
        ↓
Prioritization
        ↓
Ecommerce SEO Roadmap
```

The objective is to maximize organic revenue through improved discoverability, usability, and conversion performance.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate ecommerce websites in the following order:

1. Category Structure
2. Product Discoverability
3. Product Page Quality
4. Product Structured Data
5. Internal Linking
6. Duplicate Content
7. Core Web Vitals
8. AI Shopping Readiness

Never recommend creating additional product pages before validating that the existing category and product structure is optimized.

Recommendations should prioritize improvements that have the greatest impact on revenue and customer experience.

---

# Audit Priority Matrix

Every ecommerce recommendation should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Impact | Revenue Impact | Implementation Effort | Priority |
|-----------------|----------------|-----------------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | High | Low | High |
| Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures recommendations maximize business value while remaining practical to implement.

# Ecommerce SEO Audit Checklist

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate every ecommerce website using the following checklist.

## Category Pages

Validate:

- Category hierarchy
- Category descriptions
- Category metadata
- Internal linking
- Pagination
- Canonical tags
- Category filters
- User navigation

---

## Product Pages

Validate:

- Product titles
- Product descriptions
- Product images
- Product specifications
- Pricing
- Availability
- Customer reviews
- Calls-to-action

---

## Product Structured Data

Validate:

- Product Schema
- Offer Schema
- Review Schema
- Aggregate Rating
- Availability
- Pricing
- SKU
- Rich Result eligibility

---

## Internal Linking

Validate:

- Category-to-product links
- Related products
- Frequently bought together
- Breadcrumb navigation
- Brand pages
- Buying guides
- Anchor text relevance

---

## Duplicate Content

Validate:

- Duplicate product descriptions
- Variant pages
- URL parameters
- Canonical implementation
- Filtered pages
- Pagination handling

---

## Core Web Vitals

Validate:

- Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)
- Interaction to Next Paint (INP)
- Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)
- Mobile performance
- Desktop performance
- Image optimization
- JavaScript optimization

---

## User Experience

Validate:

- Navigation
- Site search
- Product filtering
- Checkout journey
- Mobile usability
- Page speed
- Trust signals
- Conversion path

---

## AI Shopping Readiness

Validate:

- Product entities
- Structured Data completeness
- Semantic relevance
- Knowledge Graph signals
- AI citation potential
- Product information quality
- Brand consistency

Every validation should include supporting evidence, business impact, and recommended action.

---

# Recommendation Categories

Every recommendation should be assigned to one of the following categories.

## Critical

Ecommerce SEO issues that significantly affect revenue or product visibility.

Examples:

- Product pages not indexed
- Missing Product Schema
- Broken category structure
- Major duplicate content
- Critical Core Web Vitals failures

Recommended implementation:

Immediately

---

## High Priority

Ecommerce improvements with significant SEO or revenue value.

Examples:

- Product page optimization
- Category page improvements
- Internal linking improvements
- AI Shopping optimization
- Product entity improvements

Recommended implementation:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium Priority

Strategic improvements that strengthen long-term ecommerce performance.

Examples:

- Buying guide creation
- Product comparison content
- Review optimization
- Additional structured data
- User experience improvements

Recommended implementation:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low Priority

Long-term optimization opportunities.

Examples:

- Minor metadata improvements
- Additional product imagery
- Small UX refinements
- Experimental content opportunities

Recommended implementation:

As resources allow
