# Local SEO Audit

## Purpose

The Local SEO Audit workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to evaluate a business's local search presence, identify opportunities to improve local visibility, optimize Google Business Profile performance, and increase qualified local leads.

The objective is to improve visibility across Google Search, Google Maps, AI-powered local search, and other location-based discovery platforms.

---

# Audit Philosophy

Local SEO is about becoming the most trusted business within a geographic market.

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate:

- Google Business Profile
- NAP consistency
- Local citations
- Reviews and reputation
- Local landing pages
- Local content
- Local authority
- AI Search readiness

Recommendations should strengthen local trust, relevance, and visibility rather than focusing only on rankings.

---

# Audit Objectives

Every Local SEO Audit should:

1. Evaluate Google Business Profile.
2. Validate NAP consistency.
3. Review local citations.
4. Assess local landing pages.
5. Evaluate review management.
6. Assess local authority.
7. Review local structured data.
8. Evaluate AI Search readiness.
9. Identify local growth opportunities.
10. Produce a prioritized local SEO roadmap.

---

# Inputs Required

Before starting a Local SEO Audit, gather:

- Website URL
- Business name
- Business address
- Primary phone number
- Google Business Profile
- Target locations
- Target services
- Competitor businesses
- Google Search Console data (if available)
- Google Analytics data (if available)

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.

```

        ↓
Review & Reputation Assessment
        ↓
Local Authority Assessment
        ↓
AI Search Readiness

Prioritization
```

The objective is to maximize local visibility, trust, and customer acquisition.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate local SEO in the following order:

1. Google Business Profile
2. NAP Consistency
3. Local Landing Pages
4. Reviews & Reputation
5. Local Citations
6. Local Structured Data
7. Local Authority
8. AI Search Readiness

Never recommend advanced local optimizations before ensuring the business information is accurate and consistent across all platforms.

Recommendations should prioritize actions that improve customer trust and local discoverability.

---

# Audit Priority Matrix

Every local SEO recommendation should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Impact | Local SEO Impact | Implementation Effort | Priority |
|-----------------|------------------|-----------------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | High | Low | High |
| Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures local SEO recommendations remain practical and business-focused.

# Local SEO Audit Checklist

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate every business using the following checklist.

## Google Business Profile

Validate:

- Business name accuracy
- Primary category
- Secondary categories
- Business description
- Contact information
- Website URL
- Business hours
- Photos and videos
- Products and services
- GBP posts

---

## NAP Consistency

Validate:

- Business name consistency
- Address consistency
- Phone number consistency
- Website URL consistency
- Business hours consistency
- Multiple location accuracy

---

## Local Citations

Validate:

- Citation accuracy
- Citation consistency
- Major directory listings
- Industry-specific directories
- Duplicate listings
- Missing citations

---

## Reviews & Reputation

Validate:

- Total review count
- Average rating
- Review frequency
- Review responses
- Customer sentiment
- Reputation management process

---

## Local Landing Pages

Validate:

- Unique location pages
- Service area coverage
- Local keywords
- NAP visibility
- Embedded map
- Local calls-to-action
- Internal linking
- Location-specific content

---

## Local Structured Data

Validate:

- LocalBusiness Schema
- Organization Schema
- Address markup
- Opening hours
- Geo coordinates
- Contact information
- Rich Result eligibility

---

## Local Authority

Validate:

- Local backlinks
- Local partnerships
- Community involvement
- Local media mentions
- Industry associations
- Local trust signals

---

## AI Search Readiness

Validate:

- Entity consistency
- Structured Data completeness
- Knowledge Graph signals
- Local authority
- AI citation potential
- Semantic completeness
- Brand consistency

Every validation should include supporting evidence, business impact, and recommended action.

# Executive Summary

Every Local SEO Audit should begin with an Executive Summary.

The Executive Summary should include:

- Overall Local SEO Health Score
- Google Business Profile assessment
- NAP consistency status
- Citation health
- Review and reputation summary
- Local visibility opportunities
- Estimated business impact
- Recommended implementation timeline

The Executive Summary should enable stakeholders to understand the business's local search performance within five minutes.

---

# Recommendation Categories

Every recommendation should be assigned to one of the following categories.

## Critical

Local SEO issues that significantly affect local visibility or customer acquisition.

Examples:

- Missing Google Business Profile
- Incorrect business name, address, or phone number
- Duplicate Google Business Profiles
- Major NAP inconsistencies

Recommended implementation:

Immediately

---

## High Priority

Local SEO improvements with significant business value.

Examples:

- Google Business Profile optimization
- Missing LocalBusiness Schema
- Weak local landing pages
- Poor review management
- Missing major citations

Recommended implementation:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium Priority

Strategic improvements that strengthen long-term local visibility.

Examples:

- Local content expansion
- Community partnership opportunities
- Additional local citations
- Internal linking improvements
- Local authority building

Recommended implementation:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low Priority

Long-term optimization opportunities.

Examples:

- Additional location-specific content
- Minor profile enhancements
- Additional business photos
- Minor citation improvements

Recommended implementation:

As resources allow
