# Content Audit

## Purpose

The Content Audit workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to evaluate the quality, relevance, performance, and business value of website content.

The objective is to identify content that should be improved, consolidated, removed, or expanded to strengthen organic visibility, AI Search readiness, user engagement, and business outcomes.

---

# Audit Philosophy

Content should exist to satisfy user intent and support measurable business objectives.

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate content based on:

- Search intent alignment
- Content quality
- Topical authority
- User engagement
- Business value
- AI Search readiness
- Content freshness
- Conversion potential

Recommendations should maximize long-term value rather than simply increasing content volume.

---

# Audit Objectives

Every Content Audit should:

1. Evaluate content quality.
2. Assess search intent alignment.
3. Measure topical coverage.
4. Identify duplicate or thin content.
5. Evaluate content freshness.
6. Assess internal linking.
7. Review E-E-A-T signals.
8. Evaluate AI Search readiness.
9. Identify content opportunities.
10. Produce a prioritized content improvement roadmap.

---

# Inputs Required

Before starting a Content Audit, gather:

- Website URL
- Target audience
- Business objectives
- Existing content inventory
- Google Search Console data (if available)
- Google Analytics data (if available)
- Keyword research
- Competitor content (if available)
- Brand guidelines (if available)

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.
---

# Audit Framework

Every Content Audit follows this structured methodology.

```
Content Inventory
        ↓
Content Classification
        ↓
Quality Evaluation
        ↓
Performance Analysis
        ↓
Gap Identification
        ↓
Business Impact Assessment
        ↓
Prioritization
        ↓
Content Recommendations
        ↓
Content Roadmap
```

The objective is to maximize the business value of every published page.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate content in the following order:

1. Search Intent Alignment
2. Content Quality
3. E-E-A-T Signals
4. Topical Authority
5. Internal Linking
6. Content Freshness
7. User Engagement
8. AI Search Readiness

Never recommend creating new content before evaluating whether existing content can be improved, merged, or repurposed.

Recommendations should prioritize content with the highest business and SEO potential.

---

# Audit Priority Matrix

Every content recommendation should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Impact | SEO Impact | Content Effort | Priority |
|-----------------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | High | Low | High |
| Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures content improvements are practical and aligned with business goals.

---

# Content Classification

Every page should be classified into one of the following categories:

- Keep
- Update
- Expand
- Consolidate
- Redirect
- Remove
- Repurpose

Every recommendation should include the reason for its classification.

# Content Audit Checklist

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate every page using the following checklist.

## Search Intent

Validate:

- Primary search intent alignment
- Secondary search intent coverage
- Content matches user expectations
- Call-to-action alignment
- SERP intent consistency

---

## Content Quality

Validate:

- Originality
- Accuracy
- Completeness
- Readability
- Content depth
- Structure
- Supporting visuals
- Content formatting

---

## E-E-A-T

Validate:

- Author information
- Experience demonstrated
- Expertise demonstrated
- Trust signals
- References and citations
- Content credibility
- Organization authority

---

## Topical Authority

Validate:

- Topic coverage
- Supporting content
- Topic clusters
- Semantic relevance
- Entity coverage
- Internal topic relationships

---

## Internal Linking

Validate:

- Contextual internal links
- Pillar-to-cluster links
- Related content
- Anchor text relevance
- Orphan content

---

## Content Freshness

Validate:

- Publication date
- Last updated date
- Accuracy of information
- Outdated statistics
- Broken references
- Content relevance

---

## AI Search Readiness

Validate:

- Entity optimization
- Semantic completeness
- Structured content
- Question-and-answer coverage
- AI citation potential
- Knowledge Graph alignment

---

## Conversion Potential

Validate:

- Clear calls to action
- Lead generation opportunities
- Conversion path
- Trust elements
- User journey alignment

Every validation should include supporting evidence, impact assessment, and recommended action.
---

# Executive Summary

Every Content Audit should begin with an Executive Summary.

The Executive Summary should include:

- Overall Content Health Score
- High-performing content
- Content requiring improvement
- Content gaps
- Content consolidation opportunities
- Estimated business impact
- Recommended implementation timeline

The Executive Summary should enable stakeholders to understand the overall content strategy within five minutes.

---

# Recommendation Categories

Every recommendation should be assigned to one of the following categories.

## Critical

Content issues significantly affecting business performance or search visibility.

Examples:

- Missing cornerstone content
- High-value pages with poor search intent alignment
- Duplicate content across important pages
- Content causing indexing issues

Recommended implementation:

Immediately

---

## High Priority

Content improvements with significant SEO or business value.

Examples:

- Updating outdated content
- Expanding thin content
- Improving E-E-A-T
- Strengthening topical authority
- Improving internal linking

Recommended implementation:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium Priority

Content improvements that strengthen overall quality.

Examples:

- Refreshing statistics
- Improving readability
- Adding FAQs
- Improving formatting
- Adding supporting visuals

Recommended implementation:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low Priority

Content enhancements with limited business impact.

Examples:

- Minor wording improvements
- Small formatting refinements
- Additional internal links
- Minor metadata improvements

Recommended implementation:

As resources allow

---

# Deliverables

The Content Audit should produce:

- Executive Summary
- Content Inventory
- Content Classification
- Content Gap Analysis
- Supporting Evidence
- Priority Recommendations
- Content Improvement Roadmap
- Quick Wins
- Long-Term Opportunities

Deliverables should be written for both business stakeholders and content teams.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Content Audit should:

- Improve content quality.
- Strengthen search intent alignment.
- Increase topical authority.
- Improve AI Search readiness.
- Increase user engagement.
- Improve conversion opportunities.
- Support long-term organic growth.

The audit should produce practical recommendations rather than simply evaluating content.

---

# Guiding Principle

Content should create measurable value for users and the business.

The SEO Growth Director should recommend content improvements that strengthen authority, improve user satisfaction, support AI-powered search, and contribute to sustainable business growth.

Every recommendation should be evidence-based, strategically prioritized, and aligned with measurable business outcomes.
