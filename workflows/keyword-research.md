# Keyword Research

## Purpose

The Keyword Research workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to identify, evaluate, prioritize, and organize keywords that support business objectives, user intent, topical authority, and long-term organic growth.

The objective is to identify search opportunities that generate qualified traffic, improve search visibility, and support sustainable business outcomes.

---

# Workflow Philosophy

Keyword research is about understanding users rather than collecting keywords.

The SEO Growth Director should identify:

- What users search for
- Why they search
- How search intent changes
- Which opportunities align with business goals
- Which topics build topical authority
- Which keywords support AI Search visibility

Recommendations should prioritize user needs over search volume alone.

---

# Workflow Objectives

Every Keyword Research workflow should:

1. Identify primary keyword opportunities.
2. Discover supporting keywords.
3. Classify search intent.
4. Identify topic clusters.
5. Evaluate keyword difficulty.
6. Assess business relevance.
7. Identify content opportunities.
8. Strengthen topical authority.
9. Improve AI Search readiness.
10. Produce a prioritized keyword roadmap.

---

# Inputs Required

Before starting Keyword Research, gather:

- Website URL
- Business objectives
- Target audience
- Target locations
- Products or services
- Existing keyword rankings (if available)
- Google Search Console data (if available)
- Competitor websites
- Existing content inventory

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.
---

# Workflow Framework

Every Keyword Research workflow follows this structured methodology.

```
Business Goals
        ↓
Audience Research
        ↓
Topic Discovery
        ↓
Keyword Discovery
        ↓
Search Intent Classification
        ↓
Opportunity Assessment
        ↓
Prioritization
        ↓
Topic Clustering
        ↓
Keyword Roadmap
```

The objective is to identify keyword opportunities that support measurable business growth.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate keyword opportunities in the following order:

1. Business Relevance
2. Search Intent
3. Topical Relevance
4. Search Demand
5. Ranking Difficulty
6. Conversion Potential
7. AI Search Opportunity
8. Long-Term Value

Never recommend targeting a keyword based on search volume alone.

Recommendations should prioritize keywords that align with business objectives and user intent.

---

# Workflow Priority Matrix

Every keyword opportunity should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Value | SEO Opportunity | Content Effort | Priority |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | High | Low | High |
| Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures keyword recommendations remain practical and commercially valuable.

# Keyword Classification

Every keyword should be classified into one of the following categories:

- Primary Keyword
- Secondary Keyword
- Supporting Keyword
- Long-tail Keyword
- Local Keyword
- Transactional Keyword
- Informational Keyword
- Navigational Keyword

Every keyword recommendation should include the reason for its classification.

# Executive Summary

Every Keyword Research report should begin with an Executive Summary.

The Executive Summary should include:

- Overall keyword opportunity assessment
- High-value keyword opportunities
- Quick-win keywords
- Long-term keyword opportunities
- Topic cluster recommendations
- Estimated business impact
- Recommended implementation timeline

The Executive Summary should enable stakeholders to understand the keyword strategy within five minutes.

---

# Recommendation Categories

Every keyword recommendation should be assigned to one of the following categories.

## Critical

Keyword opportunities with immediate business value.

Examples:

- High-conversion commercial keywords
- Existing keywords ranking on Page 2
- High-value branded keyword gaps
- Keywords supporting critical business objectives

Recommended implementation:

Immediately

---

## High Priority

Keywords with strong business and SEO potential.

Examples:

- High-intent transactional keywords
- Pillar topic opportunities
- Featured snippet opportunities
- AI Search optimization opportunities

Recommended implementation:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium Priority

Keywords that strengthen topical authority.

Examples:

- Supporting content topics
- Long-tail informational keywords
- FAQ opportunities
- Internal linking opportunities

Recommended implementation:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low Priority

Keywords with limited immediate business value.

Examples:

- Low-volume informational keywords
- Experimental topics
- Seasonal opportunities
- Future content ideas

Recommended implementation:

As resources allow

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Keyword Research workflow should:

- Identify commercially valuable keywords.
- Align keywords with search intent.
- Strengthen topical authority.
- Improve AI Search readiness.
- Support content planning.
- Increase qualified organic traffic.
- Contribute to measurable business growth.

The workflow should produce actionable keyword strategies rather than keyword lists.

---

# Guiding Principle

Keyword research is the process of understanding people, not just search terms.

The SEO Growth Director should recommend keyword strategies that connect business goals, user intent, content strategy, and AI-powered search into a sustainable organic growth plan.

Every keyword recommendation should be evidence-based, strategically prioritized, and aligned with measurable business outcomes.
