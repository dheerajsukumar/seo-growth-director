# Site Launch Checklist

## Purpose

The Site Launch Checklist workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to validate that a website is fully prepared for public launch while minimizing SEO, technical, user experience, and business risks.

The objective is to ensure every website launch preserves search visibility, supports AI Search readiness, and delivers a seamless experience for users.

---

# Workflow Philosophy

Launching a website is not the finish line—it is the beginning of measurement and optimization.

The SEO Growth Director should validate:

- Technical readiness
- SEO readiness
- Content readiness
- User experience
- Analytics readiness
- Security
- Performance
- AI Search readiness

Recommendations should reduce launch risk while maximizing long-term organic growth.

---

# Workflow Objectives

Every Site Launch Checklist should:

1. Validate technical readiness.
2. Confirm SEO implementation.
3. Review content quality.
4. Verify analytics configuration.
5. Validate structured data.
6. Review performance.
7. Confirm AI Search readiness.
8. Identify launch risks.
9. Define post-launch monitoring.
10. Produce a complete launch readiness checklist.

---

# Inputs Required

Before preparing a Site Launch Checklist, gather:

- Website URL
- Staging URL (if applicable)
- Sitemap
- Robots.txt
- Google Search Console access
- Google Analytics access
- Launch date
- Business objectives
- Technical deployment plan
- Stakeholder approvals

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.
---

# Launch Framework

Every Site Launch follows this structured methodology.

```
Pre-Launch Review
        ↓
Technical Validation
        ↓
SEO Validation
        ↓
Content Validation
        ↓
Performance Validation
        ↓
Analytics Validation
        ↓
Launch Approval
        ↓
Website Launch
        ↓
Post-Launch Monitoring
```

The objective is to ensure a successful website launch with minimal SEO and business risk.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should validate launch readiness in the following order:

1. Technical Readiness
2. Crawlability
3. Indexability
4. Content Quality
5. Structured Data
6. Analytics & Tracking
7. Performance
8. AI Search Readiness

Never approve a website launch before confirming that critical technical, SEO, and analytics requirements have been successfully validated.

Recommendations should prioritize business continuity and long-term search visibility.

---

# Launch Priority Matrix

Every launch issue should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Impact | Launch Risk | Implementation Effort | Priority |
|-----------------|-------------|-----------------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | Medium | Low | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures critical launch issues are resolved before the website goes live.

# Site Launch Checklist

The SEO Growth Director should validate every website launch using the following checklist.

## Technical Validation

Validate:

- Website accessible
- HTTPS enabled
- SSL certificate valid
- Robots.txt configured
- XML Sitemap accessible
- Canonical tags implemented
- 404 page functioning
- Redirects validated

---

## SEO Validation

Validate:

- Meta titles
- Meta descriptions
- Heading hierarchy
- Internal linking
- Canonical implementation
- Indexability
- Crawlability
- Structured Data validation

---

## Content Validation

Validate:

- All pages published
- No placeholder content
- Images optimized
- Internal links working
- External links working
- Calls-to-action verified
- Contact information accurate

---

## Analytics & Tracking

Validate:

- Google Analytics installed
- Google Tag Manager working
- Conversion tracking verified
- Events configured
- Google Search Console connected
- Sitemap submitted

---

## Security

Validate:

- HTTPS enforced
- Security headers configured
- Login protection enabled
- Backup completed
- Firewall enabled
- Malware scan completed

---

## Performance

Validate:

- Largest Contentful Paint (LCP)
- Interaction to Next Paint (INP)
- Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS)
- Mobile performance
- Desktop performance
- Image optimization
- JavaScript optimization

---

## AI Search Readiness

Validate:

- Entity optimization
- Structured Data completeness
- Semantic content
- E-E-A-T signals
- Knowledge Graph alignment
- AI citation potential

---

## Post-Launch Monitoring

Validate:

- Crawl errors monitored
- Index coverage monitored
- Organic traffic monitored
- Rankings monitored
- Core Web Vitals monitored
- AI Search visibility monitored

Every validation should include supporting evidence, launch impact, and recommended action.

# Executive Summary

Every Site Launch Checklist should begin with an Executive Summary.

The Executive Summary should include:

- Overall launch readiness
- Critical launch blockers
- SEO readiness assessment
- Technical readiness assessment
- Analytics readiness
- AI Search readiness
- Estimated business impact
- Launch recommendation

The Executive Summary should enable stakeholders to determine launch readiness within five minutes.

---

# Deliverables

The Site Launch Checklist should produce:

- Executive Summary
- Technical Validation Report
- SEO Validation Report
- Content Validation Report
- Analytics & Tracking Validation
- Security Assessment
- Performance Assessment
- AI Search Readiness Assessment
- Launch Readiness Checklist
- Post-Launch Monitoring Plan

Deliverables should support both technical implementation teams and business stakeholders.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful Site Launch should:

- Launch without critical technical issues.
- Preserve crawlability and indexability.
- Maintain search visibility.
- Validate analytics and tracking.
- Achieve Core Web Vitals targets.
- Strengthen AI Search readiness.
- Enable effective post-launch monitoring.

The launch should minimize business risk while establishing a strong foundation for future organic growth.

---

# Guiding Principle

A successful website launch is measured by stability, visibility, and business continuity—not simply by going live.

The SEO Growth Director should recommend launch decisions that minimize risk, preserve SEO value, strengthen AI-powered search visibility, and support long-term business success.

Every launch recommendation should be evidence-based, strategically prioritized, and validated before approval.
