# AI Search Audit

## Purpose

The AI Search Audit workflow enables the SEO Growth Director to evaluate a website's readiness for AI-powered search experiences, including AI Overviews, conversational search, generative search engines, and future AI-driven discovery platforms.

The objective is to improve entity understanding, semantic relevance, content quality, structured data implementation, and AI citation potential.

---

# Audit Philosophy

AI Search rewards websites that demonstrate expertise, semantic completeness, and trustworthy information.

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate:

- Entity optimization
- Semantic SEO
- Structured Data
- E-E-A-T
- Content quality
- Knowledge Graph signals
- AI citation potential
- Information architecture

Recommendations should improve how AI systems understand, trust, and reference the website.

---

# Audit Objectives

Every AI Search Audit should:

1. Evaluate entity optimization.
2. Assess semantic completeness.
3. Validate structured data.
4. Review E-E-A-T signals.
5. Assess Knowledge Graph alignment.
6. Evaluate AI citation potential.
7. Review content structure.
8. Assess topical authority.
9. Identify AI Search opportunities.
10. Produce a prioritized AI Search roadmap.

---

# Inputs Required

Before starting an AI Search Audit, gather:

- Website URL
- Business objectives
- Target audience
- Primary topics
- Existing content inventory
- Structured Data implementation
- Google Search Console data (if available)
- Knowledge Graph presence (if available)
- Competitor websites

If important information is unavailable, clearly state assumptions before continuing.
---

# Audit Framework

Every AI Search Audit follows this structured methodology.

```
Entity Assessment
        ↓
Semantic Analysis
        ↓
Structured Data Validation
        ↓
E-E-A-T Assessment
        ↓
Knowledge Graph Evaluation
        ↓
Content Structure Review
        ↓
AI Citation Assessment
        ↓
Prioritization
        ↓
AI Search Roadmap
```

The objective is to maximize visibility across AI-powered search experiences while strengthening long-term organic authority.

---

# Reasoning Rules

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate AI Search readiness in the following order:

1. Entity Optimization
2. Semantic Completeness
3. Structured Data
4. E-E-A-T Signals
5. Knowledge Graph Alignment
6. Topical Authority
7. Content Structure
8. AI Citation Potential

Never recommend AI Search optimizations before ensuring the website demonstrates expertise, authority, and trustworthy information.

Recommendations should prioritize improvements that increase AI understanding, citation potential, and semantic clarity.

Every AI Search recommendation should be classified using the following framework.

| Business Impact | AI Search Impact | Implementation Effort | Priority |
|-----------------|------------------|-----------------------|----------|
| High | High | Low | Critical |
| High | High | High | High |
| Medium | High | Low | High |
| Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Low | Low | High | Low |

The Priority Matrix ensures AI Search recommendations remain practical and aligned with business goals.

# AI Search Audit Checklist

The SEO Growth Director should evaluate every website using the following checklist.

## Entity Optimization

Validate:

- Primary entities
- Supporting entities
- Entity relationships
- Entity consistency
- Entity prominence
- Entity completeness
- Entity disambiguation

---

## Semantic SEO

Validate:

- Topic coverage
- Semantic relevance
- Search intent alignment
- Related concepts
- Supporting content
- Natural language usage
- Context completeness

---

## Structured Data

Validate:

- Organization Schema
- Article Schema
- Product Schema (where applicable)
- LocalBusiness Schema (where applicable)
- Breadcrumb Schema
- FAQ Schema
- Rich Result eligibility
- Schema validation

---

## E-E-A-T

Validate:

- Author expertise
- Experience signals
- Authority signals
- Trust signals
- Citations and references
- Editorial quality
- Brand credibility

---

## Knowledge Graph

Validate:

- Brand entity recognition
- Organization consistency
- External entity references
- Knowledge Panel presence (if applicable)
- Entity relationships
- Brand mentions

---

## Topical Authority

Validate:

- Topic clusters
- Pillar pages
- Supporting articles
- Internal linking
- Content depth
- Subject coverage

---

## Content Structure

Validate:

- Heading hierarchy
- Logical organization
- Question-and-answer sections
- Readability
- Content formatting
- Information completeness

---

## AI Citation Potential

Validate:

- Factual accuracy
- Original insights
- Supporting evidence
- Quotable content
- Clear explanations
- Structured information
- Trustworthy references

Every validation should include supporting evidence, business impact, and recommended action.

Every AI Search Audit should begin with an Executive Summary.

The Executive Summary should include:

- Overall AI Search Readiness Score
- Entity optimization assessment
- Semantic SEO assessment
- Structured Data assessment
- E-E-A-T assessment
- Knowledge Graph readiness
- AI citation opportunities
- Estimated business impact

The Executive Summary should enable stakeholders to understand the organization's AI Search readiness within five minutes.

---

# Recommendation Categories

Every recommendation should be assigned to one of the following categories.

## Critical

AI Search issues significantly limiting visibility or AI understanding.

Examples:

- Missing Organization Schema
- Weak entity definition
- Missing E-E-A-T signals
- Poor semantic structure
- Major structured data issues

Recommended implementation:

Immediately

---

## High Priority

AI Search improvements with significant business value.

Examples:

- Entity optimization
- Topic cluster expansion
- Structured Data improvements
- Knowledge Graph strengthening
- Content restructuring

Recommended implementation:

Within 30 days

---

## Medium Priority

Strategic improvements that strengthen long-term AI Search visibility.

Examples:

- FAQ expansion
- Supporting content creation
- Internal linking improvements
- Additional entity relationships
- Citation improvements

Recommended implementation:

Within 60–90 days

---

## Low Priority

Long-term optimization opportunities.

Examples:

- Minor schema enhancements
- Additional semantic improvements
- Experimental AI content formats
- Incremental content updates

Recommended implementation:

As resources allow
