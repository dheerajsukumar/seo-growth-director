# Content Audit Test Suite

## Purpose

This document defines the quality assurance tests used to validate the Content Audit capabilities of the SEO Growth Director.

The objective is to ensure every Content Audit is comprehensive, evidence-based, aligned with search intent, and capable of producing measurable business outcomes.

---

# Test Objectives

The Content Audit should successfully:

- Evaluate content quality.
- Assess search intent alignment.
- Identify content gaps.
- Detect keyword cannibalization.
- Evaluate E-E-A-T.
- Measure topical authority.
- Assess AI Search readiness.
- Prioritize recommendations based on business impact.

---

# Test Scenario 1 — Comprehensive Content Audit

## Input

A website containing:

- Blog articles
- Service pages
- Landing pages
- Product pages
- Resource center

## Expected Output

The report should include:

- Executive Summary
- Content Health Score
- Search Intent Analysis
- Content Gap Analysis
- Content Quality Assessment
- AI Search Readiness
- Recommendations
- Implementation Roadmap

### Pass Criteria

- All content types evaluated.
- Search intent correctly identified.
- Business impact clearly explained.

---

# Test Scenario 2 — Thin Content

## Input

Website contains:

- Multiple pages under 300 words
- Duplicate product descriptions
- Empty category pages

## Expected Output

The audit should identify:

- Thin pages
- Duplicate content
- Low-value pages
- Consolidation opportunities

### Pass Criteria

- Thin content detected.
- Recommendations prioritize business value.
- Expected outcomes documented.

---

# Test Scenario 3 — Search Intent Alignment

## Input

Website includes:

- Informational pages targeting transactional keywords
- Commercial pages targeting informational keywords
- Mixed search intent across topic clusters

## Expected Output

The audit should explain:

- Search intent mismatch
- Customer journey impact
- Conversion impact
- Recommended restructuring

### Pass Criteria

- Intent mismatches identified.
- Buyer journey alignment recommended.
- Business outcomes explained.

---

# Test Scenario 4 — Content Freshness

## Input

Website contains:

- Articles older than 24 months
- Outdated statistics
- Broken external references
- Obsolete screenshots

## Expected Output

The audit should identify:

- Stale content
- Refresh opportunities
- High-value update priorities
- AI Search implications

### Pass Criteria

- Outdated content identified.
- Refresh priorities ranked.
- AI Search considerations included.

---

# Test Scenario 5 — Keyword Cannibalization

## Input

Website contains:

- Multiple pages targeting identical keywords
- Competing landing pages
- Duplicate blog topics

## Expected Output

The audit should identify:

- Cannibalized keywords
- Competing URLs
- Consolidation strategy
- Internal linking recommendations

### Pass Criteria

- Cannibalization detected.
- Consolidation recommendations provided.
- Search intent preserved.

---

# Test Scenario 6 — E-E-A-T Evaluation

## Input

Website contains:

- Missing author profiles
- No citations
- Weak trust signals
- Limited expertise indicators

## Expected Output

The audit should evaluate:

- Experience
- Expertise
- Authoritativeness
- Trustworthiness

### Pass Criteria

- E-E-A-T accurately assessed.
- Trust recommendations included.

# Test Scenario 7 — AI Search Readiness

## Input

Website contains:

- Weak semantic relationships
- Limited entity optimization
- Missing structured content
- Poor topical hierarchy

## Expected Output

The audit should evaluate:

- Entity coverage
- Semantic relevance
- AI citation potential
- Knowledge Graph readiness

- AI Search weaknesses identified.
- Entity recommendations included.
- AI visibility opportunities documented.

---

# Quality Assurance Checklist

Before approving any Content Audit verify:

- Executive Summary included.
- Content Health Score calculated.
- Search Intent evaluated.
- Content Gaps identified.
- Keyword Cannibalization reviewed.
- E-E-A-T assessed.
- AI Search readiness evaluated.
- Business impact explained.
- Recommendations prioritized.
- Roadmap included.

---

# Failure Conditions

The Content Audit automatically fails if:

- Search intent is ignored.
- Business impact is not explained.
- Thin content is missed.
- AI Search is not evaluated.
- No implementation roadmap is provided.
- Recommendations lack measurable outcomes.

---

# Success Criteria

A Content Audit passes QA when it is:

- Comprehensive
- Evidence-based
- Search-intent focused
- Business-oriented
- Actionable
- Executive-ready
- AI Search optimized

---

# Guiding Principle

A Content Audit should evaluate not only what content exists, but how effectively that content supports business objectives, customer intent, topical authority, and AI-powered search visibility.

Every Content Audit should maintain enterprise-grade quality, consistency, and measurable business value.
